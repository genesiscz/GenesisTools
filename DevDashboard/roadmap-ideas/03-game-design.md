# ADHD diverge — Game-design frame

Vantage: treat DevDashboard as a game and the developer as a player — core loops, save-states, ambient awareness, speedruns, tasteful rewards a senior wouldn't find cringe. The 3 banned obvious answers (XP/leveling-up, achievement badges, leaderboards) are off the table on purpose.

1. **"Boss-fight mode" — the phone becomes a co-op controller when an agent gets stuck**: a stalled/looping agent escalates to your pocket as an interrupt with three big tappable "moves" (nudge / pivot / abort), turning intervention into a satisfying QTE instead of a context-switch chore. `[feature]`

2. **Ambient "idle-game" home-screen widget that earns while you're away**: a live iOS widget shows your machine's "production" (tests passing, tokens burned, containers healthy) as a tamagotchi-ish state you glance at — the loop is *checking on your colony*, not opening the app. `[feature]`

3. **Replay & "ghost" runs — scrub any agent session like a speedrun VOD, race your own past self**: every session is a deterministic save-state you can rewind, fork from a checkpoint, and overlay a faded "ghost" of the last attempt to see where this run diverged. `[moat]`

4. **Sell the transport trust as a verifiable "fair-play seal" other dev tools embed**: the E2E-provable channel becomes a licensable SDK + audit badge ("DevDashboard-Verified private") that competitors and CI vendors pay to display — monetize the proof, not just the app. `[monetization]`

5. **New audience: on-call SREs & solo founders who want "incident as a level"**: reframe pager duty as a guided encounter — the app sequences the metrics/logs/terminals you need in the order a runbook would, so a 3am incident feels like a designed mission, not a cold open. `[audience]`

6. **"Hands behind your back" challenge runs — opt-in constraints that gamify discipline**: voluntarily lock yourself to phone-only steering for an hour, or "no-rm-rf weekend," tracked as personal-best constraint runs senior engineers actually brag about (craftsmanship, not points). `[feature]`

7. **Couch co-op for pair-debugging — invite a friend's phone to spectate/co-pilot one machine with scoped, revocable, E2E-shared control**: turns a stuck session into a 2-player room where a senior can drop in, take a "move," and leave, all over the same trust-verifiable channel. `[moat]`

8. **Monetize "save-states as artifacts" — sell shareable, sanitized session replays as a portfolio/teaching format**: pro tier exports a polished, secret-scrubbed replay reel ("here's how I steered Claude to fix X") that vibecoders post like a Souls-run highlight — content marketing that sells itself. `[monetization]`
