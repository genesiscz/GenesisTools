# ADHD diverge — Competitor frame

Vantage: I am a hostile incumbent (Termius / Blink / a big-cloud monitoring suite) or an attacker trying to undercut, out-position, or sabotage the obvious DevDashboard — then each attack is inverted into a moat, a wedge, or a defensive feature DevDashboard ships first.

1. **Cryptographic "session attestation" receipts the user can hand to an auditor** — *Attack: an incumbent clones the E2E checkbox and markets "secure too," collapsing the privacy claim into a feature-parity war. Invert: ship signed, exportable transport-attestation logs (transparency-log style) so "you can prove we can't see your data" becomes a verifiable artifact, not a marketing word a competitor can copy.* `[moat]`
2. **Agent action-budget guardrails enforced from the phone** — *Attack: a rival adds read-only agent monitoring and calls it done. Invert: become the only place that can pause/kill/throttle a runaway Claude Code agent (token caps, "no git push without my tap," spend ceilings) from the lock screen — steering, not just viewing.* `[feature]`
3. **Compliance & on-call engineers at regulated shops (fintech/health/defense)** — *Attack: a SaaS competitor wins SMBs on price. Invert: target teams who legally CANNOT use a vendor-readable tunnel; the trust-verifiable transport is the only thing that passes their security review, a segment incumbents structurally can't serve.* `[audience]`
4. **"Bring-your-own-relay" marketplace + per-relay revenue share** — *Attack: a cloud incumbent undercuts on hosting cost. Invert: monetize the transport layer itself — let users/teams sell/share vetted Tailscale/cloudflared relay configs, take a cut, and the moat is the network of verified relays not the app binary.* `[monetization]`
5. **Solo founders & indie homelab operators running self-hosted agents 24/7** — *Attack: enterprise tools ignore the one-person shop. Invert: a wedge audience that runs prod from a Mac Mini in a closet and needs phone-grade incident response without an ops team — exactly who a big-cloud SKU is too heavy for.* `[audience]`
6. **Tamper-evident "what happened while I slept" replay** — *Attack: an attacker who gains the phone could silently issue destructive agent commands. Invert: every steering action and agent run is recorded into an append-only, hash-chained timeline the user reviews on wake; a forged command breaks the chain visibly, turning the attack surface into an audit feature.* `[feature]`
7. **Priced per-machine, not per-seat, with a free "panic kill-switch" tier** — *Attack: competitors price per-user and lock the emergency stop behind paywalls. Invert: never paywall the kill-switch (acquisition + trust hook), charge per monitored machine instead — aligns price with the homelab/agent-farm reality competitors mis-price.* `[monetization]`
8. **Offline-degraded local mesh when the relay/cloud is down** — *Attack: a competitor (or an outage, or a DoS on the relay) severs the phone-to-machine link and the dashboard goes dark. Invert: fall back to direct LAN/BLE peer discovery so the app keeps working with zero cloud — making a relay-targeting sabotage or any infra outage a non-event for the user.* `[feature]`
